# What's Up?

I'm actively practicing piano and seeing improvements in how quickly I can build complexer chords. Most of the time I'm still only utilising root position, but I'm working on changing that. As for writing, that's going nicely too. My first (new) short story grew to 3.2k words, after which I abandoned it for lack of quality. When I want to be productive but perhaps not creative, I have some material that I can look over to improve to maybe even post one day. And after all, I think being able to abandon what you've written temporarily and come back to it later is a great way to avoid burnout and show yourself that you are in fact not quitting, merely taking a break.

Talking about writing, I've looked over some projects-posts and changed/added to them so they are more readable and more understandable, as well as having a tighter, more proffesional tone. Maintaining some cohesivity is a big part of that as well. I'll be trying to integrate similar qualities into my blog posts. If I don't build this website with pride, effort and the highest rank of quality I can, why am I doing it in the first place? With that said, I'm also trying to get a daily blog out here for that exact reason (+ to clear my head).

Anyways, a thing that has been bugging me for a while is how incorrectly people perceive my work ethic. A lot of the reason why people think I'm my biggest critique is because I am, but I need these kinds of things. Quite honestly, I think everyone does. If you're not constantly pushing yourself to spend as much time in a "productive" manner, then you're wasting your life. And I say "productive" in quotes, because I don't mean being a workaholic, either to your job or to yourself. Enjoying your life in healthy ways, such as spending time with friends, as long as you are balancing it with a good work balance is productive to me. I think a lot of people don't get that when I say that I am aiming to "always be productive". I'm not trying to turn myself into a robot, I'm trying to make every second matter. And that is my work ethic.

#### Project Starlight

I'm getting a tattoo on the back of both hands on the exact day of my birthday. I've been waiting years for this, and I've thought much about it. Not to get them would be to dim my spark for others or for the sake of a job. Not to put them there, but in a different place on my body would do the same. As much as I hate the looks that I'll get or the fact that I know people will react critically, I know I need to do this and I've come to terms with that fact after much thought today. Just like my boss said, I'm too dependent on external approval. I'm glad he opened my eyes to that, because it played a role in my decision about the tattoo.

When I first thought of getting it, it was admittedly for exactly that: External validation. That's why I'm getting it in such a visible spot. However with all the thought about bad first proffesional impressions, people looking at me weirdly or not taking my seriously, the meaning changed into the exact opposite. Not getting them would be easier and would fit societal expectations more, really. I wouldn't have the same pressure to become more confident either. I wouldn't need to be, because I'd be quiet, and I wouldn't be myself. My lips would be sealed shut, and my sense of self-expression dormant, in the prison of my anxiety. If I don't get the tattoo exactly as I've been planning and said I would, I betray myself and Project Starlights core values.

And that's why I'm going to have a star behind both of my hands in 19 days.

#### The Stella Pique Experience

I dread the day I have to do this project. It's the same internal fight I have with the tattoo. It requires so much confidence, so much certainty in myself. I've never had that. I'm confident sure, but it's not the same as being certain. If I was certain, I'd be confident always, not only under people I know aren't a huge threat. All my life I've been basing my confidence on the amount of social momentum I have. On the first day of school after a break I'd be shy, and I'd build confidence as the hours and days went by. The principle has never changed, but the stakes have. I'm currently in a sort of break, and in about a month, I'll be forced to gain social momentum alone.

To be quite frank, it seems impossible. It has seemed impossible the last couple times the same way. I dread the day I have to face either my anxiety or the star's disappointment. I dread the day.
