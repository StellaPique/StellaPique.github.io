Always known as Project Starlight, it serves as the twin project to Project Star, completing the couplet of self-improvement within Project SP.

##### Design

Originally, Starlight shared its design language with Project Star. While Star used blue as its accent colour, Starlight adopted a raspberry accent. Its primary identifier was a four-pointed star.

##### Purpose

Project Starlight is about building an identity, romanticising life without losing touch with reality, and developing the courage to truly enjoy living. It is not only about learning to appreciate yourself, but also about loving yourself and taking pride in the person you have become.

A simple question sits at the heart of the project: if you encountered yourself as a character in a story, would you admire who they are? If not, what would you want to change?

By reflecting on the character you are, from your looks, to your mannerisms, the way you speak and act towards people and the world in general, you are able to shift better to the path you want to become. If you notice that people are afraid of you because of how quiet you are, or how rude you are to some people (even if they might deserve it in your eyes) you can steer yourself in another direction. Not everyone knows you as deeply as you do, and influencing how people perceive you before they know you can make your life better. Whether that means to build the habit of smiling at strangers so they're not afraid to ask for directions, or learning not to insult others in arguments to change from the person you don't want to be anymore, that is Starlight.

##### Core Values

The core values of Starlight are pride, living life to its fullest, and helping others become their favourite selves. This includes learning new hobbies, as it is essentially impossible to become who you're meant to become without passions and hobbies that change the way you see and enjoy life. artistic hobbies are encouraged most.

##### CQ-X

CQ-X focuses on external presentation and personal style. It is based on the belief that how you dress and appear influences both how others perceive you and how you perceive yourself. The most drastic change I've made with a CQ was dyeing my hair red, which is unusual for a dude to say the least. At least I personally haven't met anyone with red hair before, excluding a girl, and my mother who gave me the idea. Following the same principle, it did change the way people perceive me. People are less afraid of me than when I dressed all black and spoke to no one. Of course me being extroverted is the biggest help, but my appearance matters just as much when I don't have the guts to talk, such as among strangers in public.

##### The Stella Pique Show

As corny as it sounds, this might become my biggest project ever, or at least one of the biggest. TSPS is supposed to launch around/on the 21-31st of July 2026, and be a social/vlogging project. It's just going to be me walking the streets of Munich and other crowded places and talking to people or messing with people lightheartedly. Of course as someone with a history of extreme anxiety, it won't just be extremely difficult, but another level of hardship beyond that to go out filming myself and socialising with strangers essentially. However, it will definitely be worth it. This will also not be the only thing I do, because I also aim to volunteer, feed the homeless (but definitely not film that part), and more on the side. I have much to gain from it identity-wise, and the world has much to gain from a full-potential Stella Pique.
