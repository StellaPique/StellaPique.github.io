Also known as PM from back when we were secretive about it.

Project Mayhem was the first project I ever started, originally created as a self-improvement initiative. Its initial goal was to help me improve my life and become someone capable of building meaningful relationships. Over time, however, its focus shifted toward helping both myself and others through difficult periods and encouraging long-term personal growth. The project included a couple meetings, internal documentation, a public Instagram self-improvement account under the name Quill Shotwell, and several smaller initiatives. More than 95% of the project's work was completed by me.

Project Mayhem centered around community and accountability. Members shared their daily successes, setbacks, and personal notes with one another, creating an environment where people could support each other, exchange advice, and track their progress together.

A role system was introduced and expanded over time. Initially there were only two roles: myself as the administrator and everyone else as members. The lowest member rank was eventually named Consignee, while my own role became Major. Additional ranks were added later, although only myself and one former friend ever reached the next tier, and the highest rank remained unclaimed. There was an estimate of about 8 Majors in the span of Project Mayhem, who all served to fill a kind of "elder" role, warming up newcomers when we got any. At the height of the project, there were about 30 people.

Eventually, I became discouraged by the lack of long-term support and the fact that nearly all organizational work depended on me. As a result, I ended Project Mayhem and initiated Project Ascend. A better, bigger and badder self-improvement community project.

##### Project Ascend

![Ascend](../../assets/ProjectAscendBanner.png)

Project Ascend was intended to be a stricter successor to Project Mayhem, with higher expectations for commitment and participation. As a result, significantly fewer people joined.

At the time, I was eager to make meaningful changes in my own life, but many of the people around me did not share the same level of commitment. Because of this, Project Ascend remained short-lived and was eventually replaced by Project Syndicate.

There is little to say about Project Syndicate, as it ultimately faced the same problems and was discontinued as well. In response, Project Mayhem II was created as another alternative, this time designed to be less strict and easier to follow. By then, however, most former members had lost their motivation, and very few returned.

##### Project Star

After repeatedly realizing that the people around me had different goals and levels of commitment than I did, I decided to stop building projects around communities and instead created **Project Star**.

Project Star was designed as a primarily solo project. While I have occasionally considered bringing other people into it, those attempts have consistently shown that the same incompatibilities still exist. Rather than forcing those collaborations to work, Project Star embraces the idea of progressing independently.

A more detailed overview of Project Star can be found in its dedicated project page [here](https://stellapique.github.io/projects/project-star/).
